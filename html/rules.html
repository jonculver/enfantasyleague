Fantasy Football is a game of luck and skill in which you pick a team of players from the English Premiership and score points according to the performance of those players in the matches each week. Knowledge of football is helpful but by no means necessary.
In the ordinary newspaper version you can choose any player from a set price list and the same player can appear in multiple teams. Our version works somewhat differently. Twice a season we hold an auction during which players are sold to the highest bidder.
This means that firstly each player can only appear in one person's team and secondly players have no set price, with some going for massively inflated prices and others picked up relatively cheaply. It means that tactics come into play during the auction along with some measure of strategy in terms of how much money you save for the second half of the season.
Once the season is up and running the manager's job is to select a team of 11 out of the squad of 17. You can either attempt to finesse by switching players in and out each week according to the opposition they face or just leave your team alone and only switch it around if one of them is injured.

In addition to the 11 players in the team your squad also contains 6 substitutes who can be from any position. You are free to rotate these in and out of the team during the season or leave them to warm the bench as you see fit.
You cannot have more than two players from a particular club in your squad. The club a player belongs to is determined by the club he is playing for when purchased (i.e. if a player moves to a club you already have 2 from you can still keep him).
Points are scored in all Premiership matches as follows:
All players
Goal3 points
Assist2 points
Goalkeeper and Defenders
Appearance (45+ mins)1 point
Clean Sheet (75+ mins)2 points
Goal conceded-1 point
Only members of your first 11 score points. Substitutions between squad members can be made at any time during the week but do not take effect until Saturday morning. Thus any players you substitute in during the week will start scoring points in matches played on the following Saturday.
Initial selection of players is by auction (usually in the pub). You are given an initial budget of 100 million from which to buy your players.
During the mid-season transfer window in January a second auction is held and new teams are picked for the second half of the season (it doesn't correspond to actual football but auctions are fun and it's an excuse for another evening in the pub :)). In the second auction you get an injection of 50 million plus any money left over from the start of the season up to a maximum of 100 million.
During the auction managers take it in turns to "raise" a player, stating an initial bid for the player. If no-one else bids for the player then the manager who raised them gets the player at the price offered. If other managers are interested in the player then bidding continues until the final, highest bid is received, with the player going to the manager who made that bid.
The minimum price for a player in the auction is 0.1M. Minimum increments when bidding are as follows. This helps to keep the auction moving by preventing repeated small increments

Those who have spent all their money without completing their quota of 17 players can draft in players on free transfers at the end of the auction in round-robin fashion, starting with the person who ran out of money first.
You can bid for players who do not currently play in the premiership in the hope that they will be bought by a premiership team. Players bought in this way are assigned a sensible position for the purposes of the auction but until they sign for a premiership team they can only be a substitute. You cannot put these players in your team and you must be able to field a valid formation without them. You cannot play a non-registered player as a goalkeeper or in defence to get a certain 0 pts.
To avoid the auction from going on for too long only a first 11 plus maybe 1 sub are bid for on the night, depending on the number of participants. Remaining substitutes are picked up via sealed bids the day after each auction.
Each manager submits a sealed list of bids for players stating how much he is willing to pay for the player. If the manager has funds the minimum bid is 0.1M. Bids from managers with no money will be processed in round-robin fashion at the end of the sealed auction and they will pick up the players for free.

Once all the lists of bids have been received they will be processed as follows. In order of league position starting from the bottom, the first player on the first list will be sold to the manager who bid the most for that player. Then the first (unhandled) player from the second manager's list is dealt with, and so on until everyone has got their required number of players in that position or no more players are left.
Once the subs auction is over and everyone has a complete team it is possible to trade players for either players or (fantasy) cash or both from other managers. The league chairman must approve the deal to ensure no underhand behaviour or dodgy dealings.
Multiple trades may be made but at the end of it everyone involved must end up with a full compliment of players and comply with the 2-players-per-club rule.
In practice it is rare to find a situation where a mutually beneficial trade can be made, so in many seasons no transfers will be made. However the option is there if needed.
What players should I buy or avoid?
How good a player is in real life does not necessarily correspond to their ability in fantasy football. Some rules of thumb:
  1. Defensive midfielders suck. They get few goals and assists and they don't get points for clean sheets either.
  2. Good defenders from bad teams are bad buys. Defenders score points when the team doesn't concede. Therefore you want to look for players from clubs that keep things tight at the back which usually means the top clubs.
  3. Unless a player has been injured or moved clubs past performance is a very good indicator. This data is available before and during the auction
What tactics should I employ in the auction?
In general any pre-planned strategies tend to go out of the window quickly once the auction gets going but there are some tactics that are worth bearing in mind:
  1. Towards the end of the auction many people will be inelligable to buy certain players either because they already have 2 from that club or because they are full in that position or cannot afford them. Not raising the player you want in the hope of picking them up cheaply at the end is a common occurrence. Conversely people often raise the best players early to prevent someone getting a bargain.
  2. Don't listen when Simon tells you a player is injured or when John says they are going to move to Spurs.
How much do players sell for?
This varies a lot depending on the stage of the auction and the groupthink of the people involved. The top strikers and midfielders often go for 15-20M or sometimes even more. In January 2014 after scoring more than double the points of everyone else in the first half of the season Luis Suarez was sold for 46M.
Most decent players go fall into the 5-10M range while those people don't rate may go for 2 or less. Towards the end of the auction players will be raised that no one can or wants to bid for and they will be picked up for the minimum 0.1 regardless of how good they are.
For sealed bids the remaining players are generally pretty medeocre and most go for less than 1M, often the minimum 0.1
Can I fire players I bought in the auction but do not want?
No. Any players bought in the auction must be kept. It is possible to transfer them in exchange for a player from someone else's team if you can find someone willing to take them.
